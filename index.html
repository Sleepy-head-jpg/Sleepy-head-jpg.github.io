Hey, thanks for dropping by! Here's a bit about me.

I'm a master's student studying community ecology in Himalayan forests ‚Äî specifically how mixed-species bird flocks organise themselves and what that reveals about ecosystem health. But I've always been more naturalist than scientist: I notice a feather on a trail, an unfamiliar call from the canopy, a footprint in mud at first light. That habit of slow attention is what became my research.

Outside the work, I bird obsessively, photograph everything that holds still long enough, and write field notes that don't always fit a methods section. The Anecdotes page is where those live. Have a look around!
